About this Site

This site facilitates and automates cybersecurity competitions for Dakota State University's DefSec and OffSec clubs. HackerNet was written to be as simple and fast as possible. It is open source and can be found at (GITHUB URL).

About Learning

The Learn tab contains interactive, hands-on micro-courses (μCourses?) put together by DSU's Defensive Security Club. Each one is comprised of a vApp, a PDF, Slides, and (if presented) a video recording. The accompanying scoring engine in most courses will give visual feedback if the goal of each course has been accomplished.

About Deploying

The Deploy tab will deploy a specified vApp to your account. It is a simple alternatve to the IALab's interface.

King of the Hill

The King of the Hill (KOTH) game mode is both offensive and defensive. HackerNet gives the IP of one vulnerable machine to (up to) six competitors, who attempt to hack into the machine and maintain access while patching vulnerabilities, submitting flags, and obtaining the root or Administrator account to put their username in /root/king.txt or C:\Users\Administrator\king.txt to gain "king points." A scoring server is checking the box's services-- if half or more ( >= 50% ) of the box's services are down, king points will not be awarded.

KOTH Rules
  • Breaking into the Machine
    • Attacking DSU IALAB infrastructure or anything other than the provided IP address is forbidden.
    • The king scoring service on port 9999 is out of scope, otherwise any method to obtain code execution on the system is allowed.
  • On the Machine
    • Removing or modifying the or permissions of flags is forbidden.
    • Blocking IPs or whitelisting your own IP is forbidden.
    • Killing other user's sessions is allowed (if not scripted).
    • Deleting, removing, or crippling services, files, and users are allowed, however one must be wary of failing service checks and losing the ability to gain king points.
